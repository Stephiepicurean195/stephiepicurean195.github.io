📋 What is this tool?

This software helps you move data from your website forms to a Google Sheet automatically. You do not need to copy and paste data manually anymore. It works with common WordPress plugins like Elementor, Gravity Forms, and Contact Form 7. The program listens for new form entries and sends them to your spreadsheet in real-time.

⚙️ System Requirements

Your computer needs to meet these basic standards to run the application:

  • Windows 10 or Windows 11.
  • At least 4GB of RAM.
  • An active internet connection to send data to Google.
  • A Google account to store your files.

🔗 Linking Your Forms

The program uses something called a Webhook to talk to your website. A Webhook acts like a bridge for data.

  1. Open the application from your desktop.
  2. Log in with your credentials.
  3. Open your WordPress dashboard in a web browser.
  4. In your form plugin settings, look for "Webhooks" or "Integrations."
  5. Copy the link provided inside the application.
  6. Paste this link into the webhook field in your WordPress plugin.
  7. Save your settings in WordPress.

📊 Using Your Google Sheet

Ensure your Google Sheet is ready to receive data.

The application needs permission to write to your files. When you start the software for the first time, a web page will open. This page asks you to confirm that the app can access your Google Sheets. Click "Allow" to finish the connection.

Any test entries you submit through your website will now appear in your spreadsheet automatically.

🛡️ Security Information

Data privacy matters. This tool only moves the information you choose to share. It does not store your WordPress login details or your full account password. All data sent to Google uses encrypted connections to keep your information safe from outside eyes.

💡 Common Questions

Does this work if my computer is off? No. The application must run on your computer to process the incoming data.

What if the data does not appear in the sheet? Check that your internet connection is stable. Also, ensure your WordPress plugin is active and configured correctly with the webhook URL.

Can I use this for multiple forms? Yes. You can set up as many connections as you need within the application dashboard.
